Side Pot Calculator

Calculate side-pot values based on the amount players have bet and their ranking

Use our handy side-pot calculator to easily work out how much each player has won when multiple players have bet different amounts. Side pots occur when one or more players go 'all-in' within a round, but their total stack cannot match the largest bet placed by another player. With up to nine players at a table these calculations can get pretty complex.

Enter each player's total bet and a value to indicate where they came in the overall ranking below. The higher the rank, the better their hand is relative to the other players. The highest rank equates to the best hand. If two players have the same rank they will split the relevant side-pot.

How does it work?

How side pots are calculated

As mentioned above, side pots occur when one or more players cannot bet as much as an opponent - this can happen quite often in 'No Limit' Texas Hold'em as a player is free to go all-in at any stage.

For example; Bob has a really good hand, but only has £1.50 left in his stack, whereas everyone else left in the round has more. 'No Limit' is a very fair game in this regard as the player can bet their whole stack (1.50) and stay in the round.

Jane also thinks she has the best hand, and therefore bets all of her remaining stack (£3.20).

Dave is the only player left to bet, and could bet more than both Jane and Bob, but its pointless as £3.20 is the maximum he can win off of anyone else (Jane). Poker Rooms Now will automatically cap a player's bet to the largest stack of any other player.

So the River come out and Bob has the top hand - good for them. But, as he could only bet £1.50, that's the maximum he can win off of the other players; so he takes £4.50 - £1.50 off of each player including his original bet.

Jane has the second best hand and can therefore take a maximum of £3.20, less what they have already given to Bob (£1.70). As Dave could only match Jane's bet, then he lost and therefore Jane takes a total of £3.40.

If there was a fourth player with £10.50, then Dave could have bet up to that amount and the number of side pots could have increased further, dependent on who had the higher hand. It is also possible for two players to draw on one or more side pots if they have the same ranking. If the winning player has also put in the most money (or equal to the most), then they win all of the side-pots.

This is where Poker Rooms Now makes homes games simpler - we handle all of the side-pot calculations - obviously this is good news, as working out multiple side pots at 1:00am can be hazardous!
