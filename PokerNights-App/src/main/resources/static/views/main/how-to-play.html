Getting Started

The basic game-play of Texas Hold'em

Texas Hold'em poker is simple to start playing, but can take years to master, which is probably why it is one of the most popular card games in the world.

The basic rules and game-play are pretty simple;

  • Each player at the table is dealt two cards - hole cards. These are private cards that no-one should see until you need to prove if you've won.
  • Through the game five 'community cards' are dealt face-up on the table.
  • The aim is to make the best set of five cards from the five on the table and the two in your hand.
  • The player with the best five cards wins the game and the pot! For a list of the hand rankings, please see Hand Ranks below.

A bit more detail...?

The skill (and the fun) in Texas Hold'em is all about the betting. There are four betting rounds which are linked to the community cards being dealt;

  • Pre-flop: Prior to any player being dealt their cards, blinds (an ante) most be provided by the two players to the left of the dealer - this is the small blind and big blind respectively. The big blind is always double the small blind. Essentially, this is the cost of receiving your cards and stimulates 'the action'.
    After the big blind is posted, all players are dealt their hole cards. For a player to stay within the round they must bet at least the same amount as the big blind (but they can raise the stake as well) The first person to choose what they bet is the player to the left of the big blind.
  • Flop: Once the pre-flop bets are all the same (equalized), they're collected in to the game pot and the 'flop' is dealt. The Flop is the first three community cards dealt face-up in the center of the table, all at the same time. Once the flop is visible, there is a round of betting, starting with the first player to the dealer's left. Again, the round is only complete when the bets on the table are all equal. This is generally the first stage where you may see one or more player's fold their hand, because it's the first time everyone can really assess the probability of their hand coming out top.
  • Turn: This is the penultimate community card to be dealt. We have another round of betting based on this card being shown.
  • River: The final community card and the last round of betting takes place. When the bets have equalised, the last person to be 'called' must show their cards to prove whether they've won or not. (More on Poker Terms below). Obviously if the person doing the 'calling' has actually got a better hand, then they have to prove it by turning their cards over as well.
    The player with the best hand wins the pot.
  • Sometimes, dependent on the game variant, it's not as simple as the winner receiving the total pot value. For a more in-depth look at the potential scenarios, take a look at breaking a tie or in the case of different bet values, our Side Pot Calculator.

Poker Terms

A quick run-down of poker terminology

We've already had a run down of the terms, or names of, the card rounds above, so here's a quick check list of other popular terms;

  • Check: Quite simply, a zero-bet. You can only check if you are the first player to bet, or if all preceding players have checked.
  • Call: When betting, a 'call' is to match the preceding player's bet.
  • Raise: To increase the value of the bet above that of the preceding player. Bets after a raise must match or exceed the raise value.
  • Fold or Muck: Is to forfeit your hand without showing your cards.
  • Reveal: To show your hole cards. Note that at PokerRoomsNow if you Reveal before the game winner is decided then this is the same as Fold, but showing your cards in the process (just so you scare your friends).
  • Stack: The amount of money you have available at the table to bet with.
  • Buy-in: This is the initial stake you pay to sit at the table. The money is a part of your Stack to bet with, but is the minimum you can turn up with.
  • Cash-out: Collect all your money on the table and leave.
  • Wallet: At PokerRoomsNow we have a 'Game Wallet' - this is the amount of money you can draw down from to fund your Buy-in and any re-buys required during a game. Currently there is no facility to increase your wallet once it is exhausted.
  • Rake: This is a fee paid to the dealer/ casino based on the pot size in each round of a game. PokerRoomsNow does not have a rake fee, so you always know how much a game is going to cost before playing (Yay!)
